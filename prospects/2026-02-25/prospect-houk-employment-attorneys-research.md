## Employment Law Context

**Employment Law Characteristics:**
- High-compliance practice with strict deadlines
- 180-day filing deadline for EEOC discrimination complaints
- 180 days for state Civil Rights Division (CRD) complaints
- Complex federal/state law landscape (ACRA, ADA, Civil Rights Act of 1964, ADEA, NLRA, FLSA, Equal Pay Act, Pregnancy Discrimination Act, FMLA)
- Documentation-heavy (evidence, patterns of behavior, witnesses)
- Long litigation cycles require ongoing client communication
- Dual client types: employers (B2B) and employees (B2C)

**Pain Points Indicators:**
1. **Strict deadlines emphasized in FAQ** - "How long do I have to file?" frequently asked question
2. **"We serve as counsel to employers, executives, and employees"** - Complex client management
3. **"Dedicated to providing exceptional representation as efficiently as possible"** - Efficiency is priority
4. **Multiple practice areas** - Different workflows for each
5. **45 years combined experience** - Suggests established firm with traditional processes
6. **"Case results" from 2024** - Active caseload, ongoing need for case management

**Automation Opportunities:**
1. Deadline tracking (EEOC, CRD, statute of limitations)
2. Client intake and consultation scheduling
3. Evidence collection and organization
4. Client communication and status updates
5. Compliance management across federal/state laws
6. Case documentation workflow
7. Settlement tracking
8. Billing and invoicing
