## Pain Points Identified

### Manual Dispatch (High Probability)
**Evidence:**
- "Truck dispatched within 2 hours" promise suggests manual coordination
- 24/7 operation requires constant dispatch availability
- Testimonials praise dispatch communication (implies manual effort valued)
- Regional carriers typically use manual dispatch in smaller operations

**Industry Reality:**
- Phone-based dispatch
- Whiteboards for truck/driver assignment
- Manual driver location tracking
- Paper-based load assignments

### Customer Status Updates (High Probability)
**Evidence:**
- Testimonials consistently praise updates and communication
- "real-time updates" mentioned but no customer portal
- Customers likely call for status (manual effort)

**Industry Reality:**
- Dispatcher manually updating customers
- Phone calls for status checks

- Labor-intensive communication

**Evidence:**
- Testimonials mention dispatchers and drivers
- 24/7 operations require constant communication

## Size Estimates

### Employee Count
**Evidence:**
- Multiple dispatchers mentioned (plural in testimonials)
- Drivers referenced as team (implying more than one)
- 24/7 operation requires at least 3 shifts of dispatchers
- Dock freight service suggests warehouse crew
- Multiple truck types indicate fleet

**Estimate:** 15-20 employees
- 1-2 owners/managers
- 4-6 dispatchers (24/7 coverage)
- 8-12 drivers and warehouse staff

### Revenue Estimate
**Methodology:**
- Regional carrier with 6-state coverage
- Urgent freight typically commands premium rates
- 24/7 operation indicates volume
- Same-day freight rates: $2-4 per mile average
- Assuming 10-15 trucks average 1000 miles/day = 10,000-15,000 miles/day
- $20K-60K/day revenue = $6K-18K/month revenue

**Estimate:** $1M - $2M annual revenue
